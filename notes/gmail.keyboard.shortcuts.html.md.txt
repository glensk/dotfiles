<div class="tip">
<p><strong>Tip</strong>: Press <strong>?</strong> in Gmail to see a list of keyboard shortcuts.</p></div>

<p>Some keyboard shortcuts can be used immediately, and others need to be enabled before you can use them.</p>

<h3>Shortcuts that are always turned on</h3>

<p>These navigational and formatting shortcuts all work whether or not you've enabled the keyboard shortcuts setting.</p>

<h4>Use arrow keys to view messages and start composing</h4>

<p>While viewing your messages, use your keyboard’s arrow keys to move your cursor to a conversation, label or the compose button. Press <strong>Enter</strong> to select.</p>

<h4>Move between messages in a conversation</h4>

<p>While viewing a conversation, use <strong>n</strong> and <strong>p</strong> to go the next and previous messages in a conversation thread. Hit <strong>Enter</strong> to open or collapse a message.</p>

<h4>Navigate in compose</h4>

<p>Use the following combinations of keys when you have compose windows or chats open.</p>

<p>To format your text quickly, check out these <a href="/mail/answer/8260" target="_blank">formatting shortcuts</a>.</p>

<h3>Shortcuts that need to be turned on</h3>

<p>Before you can use these keyboard shortcuts, you’ll need to enable keyboard shortcuts in your settings:</p>

<ol class="spaced-list">
<li>Click the <img src="//www.google.com/help/hc/images/mail/mail_gear.png"> gear in the top right and select <strong>Settings</strong>.</li>
<li>Find the "Keyboard shortcuts" section and select <strong>Keyboard shortcuts on</strong>.</li>
<li>Click <strong>Save Changes</strong> at the bottom of the page.</li>
</ol>
